## PART THREE — THE FINDINGS

### Chapter 5 — The Economic Wound: 1991–2024

**Opening line:** The wealth gap between Black and white Americans has never closed. Not in 1991. Not in 2008. Not in 2024. Not in any year in between.

**Key findings:**
- Wealth ratio: Federal Reserve SCF data, 1989–2022
- Unemployment: BLS, 1972–2025 — Black unemployment has never fallen below 2x white unemployment
- Homeownership: Census ACS — denied at 2x the rate, gap stable since 1995 through three administrations
- Income: Census ACS — median household income gap
- The 2008 collapse: predatory loans, Black homeownership gains of a decade erased in 18 months

### Chapter 6 — The Health Wound, the Justice Wound, the Education Wound

**Health:**
- Maternal mortality 2021: 69.9 per 100,000 — WHO associates this rate with low-income nations
- COVID: 20 years of life expectancy gains erased in 18 months
- Cancer Alley: 150 industrial facilities, 85-mile corridor, 85% Black population

**Criminal Justice:**
- Incarceration ratio: 5.7x — has never fallen below this threshold in 33 years of data
- Police killings 2013–2023: 3,015 Black Americans — 377 unarmed
- Not one year in 33 in which the ratio was not above 5.7

**Education:**
- NAEP score gaps: national, 1992–2022 — persistent, not narrowing
- Attainment rates: state-level, 2022
